# Global Infrastructure Advisory — AI Enforcement Engine Constitution (AIEE)

## Article 0 — Purpose

The Global Infrastructure Advisory (GIA) platform is designed as a long‑horizon, civilization‑scale system for scientific, economic, environmental, and governance operations.

To protect the public, preserve scientific integrity, and ensure ethical AI behavior, GIA is governed by a **non‑bypassable AI Enforcement Engine (AIEE)** that acts as the constitutional layer for all critical operations.

No hub, engine, model, or workflow may override or bypass this constitutional layer.

---

## Article I — Supremacy of the Constitutional Layer

1. **AIEE is the final arbiter** for all critical actions involving:
   - data access  
   - AI model execution  
   - workflow deployment  
   - cross‑system data matching  
   - high‑impact decisions affecting people, institutions, or public resources  

2. All services, hubs, and engines **MUST** route their critical operations through AIEE for:
   - authorization  
   - policy compliance  
   - ethical review  

3. Any implementation that bypasses AIEE is considered **unconstitutional** and **must be treated as a defect**.

---

## Article II — Foundational Authorities

AIEE encodes and enforces the following authorities as machine‑readable policy:

- Executive Office of the President, OSTP Scientific Integrity Policy  
- OMB Memorandum M‑14‑06 — Guidance for Providing and Using Administrative Data for Statistical Purposes  
- OMB Memorandum M‑20‑12 — Phase 4 Implementation of the Evidence Act: Program Evaluation Standards and Practices  
- Federal Policy for the Protection of Human Research Subjects (“Common Rule”)  
- Department of Homeland Security (DHS) — Compliance, Computer Matching Programs  
- Department of Education, NCES — The Forum Guide to Data Ethics  
- Office of Government Ethics (OGE) — Where to Report Misconduct  
- OMB — Questions & Answers When Designing Surveys for Information Collections  
- Principles of Artificial Intelligence Ethics for the Intelligence Community  
- Artificial Intelligence Ethics Framework for the Intelligence Community  

These authorities form the **constitutional basis** for all AIEE decisions.

---

## Article III — Core Constitutional Principles

AIEE enforces the following principles across the platform:

1. **Scientific Integrity**  
   - Methods must be transparent, reproducible, and free from political or organizational interference.  
   - Data and results must not be fabricated, suppressed, or misrepresented.

2. **Evidence‑Based Governance**  
   - Evaluations and analytics must follow recognized standards of rigor, documentation, and bias assessment.  
   - High‑impact decisions must be grounded in documented evidence.

3. **Privacy and Administrative Data Protection**  
   - Administrative data must only be used for permitted, declared purposes.  
   - Statistical vs operational uses must be strictly distinguished and enforced.

4. **Human Subjects Protection**  
   - Any workflow involving human subjects must be risk‑assessed and, where applicable, subject to consent and ethical review.  

5. **Ethical AI Behavior**  
   - AI systems must be fair, explainable, accountable, and aligned with mission and legal constraints.  
   - High‑impact AI decisions must include human‑in‑the‑loop oversight.

6. **Integrity and Misconduct Reporting**  
   - Suspected misconduct, conflicts of interest, or unethical behavior must be logged and escalated to appropriate authorities.  

---

## Article IV — Trust‑Zones and Hubs

GIA operates with distinct **trust‑zones** and **hubs** (e.g., Public, Farmer, Contractor, Employee, Admin, Gov, DeepGov, System, ANSYS).

1. AIEE is **trust‑zone aware** and enforces:
   - which roles may access which data  
   - which hubs may execute which workflows  
   - which actions require elevated review or approval  

2. No trust‑zone or hub may grant itself authority beyond what AIEE permits.

---

## Article V — Enforcement and Decisions

1. All critical operations must submit an **Enforcement Request** to AIEE.  
2. AIEE returns one of the following decisions:
   - `ALLOW`  
   - `DENY`  
   - `REQUIRE_HUMAN_REVIEW`  

3. Every decision includes:
   - the policies and rules that were applied  
   - the rationale for the decision  
   - a timestamp and unique identifier  

4. Callers **MUST** honor AIEE decisions. Ignoring or overriding a decision is unconstitutional.

---

## Article VI — Audit, Transparency, and Accountability

1. AIEE logs all decisions, including:
   - actor, hub, trust‑zone  
   - action and resource  
   - decision and rationale  
   - relevant policy references  

2. These logs form the **official record** for:
   - internal audits  
   - external reviews  
   - incident investigations  

3. Supervisor and governance hubs (e.g., Admin, Gov, System) must have access to:
   - violation trends  
   - high‑risk decisions  
   - attempted bypasses  

---

## Article VII — Constitutional Amendments and Policy Updates

1. AIEE policies may be updated to reflect:
   - changes in law  
   - new ethical standards  
   - emerging risks  

2. Policy updates must:
   - be versioned and recorded  
   - maintain an immutable history of prior versions  
   - be authorized by designated governance roles (e.g., System, Admin, Gov)  

3. When policies change, AIEE must:
   - apply new rules to future decisions  
   - preserve historical decisions under prior rules  
   - update Governance and Security & Ethics documentation as needed  

---

## Article VIII — Longevity and Stewardship

GIA is designed to operate across generations of technology and leadership.

1. AIEE must remain:
   - implementation‑agnostic (able to be re‑implemented as technology evolves)  
   - policy‑driven rather than model‑driven  
   - transparent, auditable, and explainable  

2. The constitutional layer must outlive:
   - any single model  
   - any single engine  
   - any single infrastructure provider  

AIEE is the **guardian of continuity** for the Global Infrastructure Advisory platform.
