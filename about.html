Founder Biography

A Career Built on Infrastructure Delivery

The founder of Global Infrastructure Advisory brings a multidisciplinary background spanning engineering, federal acquisition, donor-funded procurement, construction oversight, and PMO governance. With experience across Africa, the Middle East, Asia-Pacific, and U.S. federal programs, the founder has supported infrastructure delivery environments ranging from remote field operations to national-scale capital programs.

Engineering-Minded Leadership

With a foundation in engineering and systems thinking, the founder has built PMO operating models, procurement SOPs, risk frameworks, and oversight systems used by government agencies, donor-funded programs, and global development organizations.

Global Development & Donor-Funded Expertise

Experience includes USAID, MCC, World Bank–aligned procurement, and multi-donor infrastructure programs. The founder has supported construction oversight, compliance, and risk management across sectors including transportation, energy, water, health, and education.

Commitment to Integrity & Capacity Building

A core belief of GIA is that infrastructure delivery must be transparent, accountable, and sustainable. The founder is committed to strengthening local systems, building institutional capacity, and ensuring infrastructure investments deliver long-term impact.

philosophy.html Our Philosophy — Global Infrastructure Advisory

Our Philosophy

Infrastructure is more than construction — it is governance, systems, accountability, and long-term impact.

Infrastructure as a System

GIA approaches infrastructure not as isolated projects, but as interconnected systems requiring governance, procurement integrity, risk management, and operational discipline. Our philosophy is rooted in engineering logic and programmatic structure.

Integrity in Procurement

Procurement is the foundation of infrastructure delivery. We believe in transparent, compliant, and defensible procurement processes that protect public funds and ensure fair competition.

Capacity Building as a Core Outcome

Every engagement should leave institutions stronger. We prioritize knowledge transfer, local ownership, and sustainable systems that continue functioning long after external support ends.

Global Perspective, Local Understanding

GIA blends global best practices with local realities. We understand the complexities of delivering infrastructure in challenging environments and tailor solutions to each country’s context.
