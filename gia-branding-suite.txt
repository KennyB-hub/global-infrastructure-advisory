<section class="border border-slate-800 bg-slate-900/60 rounded-xl p-6">
  <h3 class="text-sm font-semibold text-slate-100">OSA Standard for Subcontractors</h3>
  <p class="mt-2 text-xs text-slate-400">
    The Operational Standards Agreement (OSA) defines the minimum technical, safety, documentation, and
    performance requirements for all subcontractors operating under Global Infrastructure Advisory frameworks.
    These standards ensure consistency, transparency, and engineering-grade delivery across all sectors.
  </p>

  <ul class="mt-4 space-y-1 text-xs text-slate-400">
    <li>• Compliance with PMO governance, reporting, and documentation protocols</li>
    <li>• Mandatory QC/QA evidence submission for all works</li>
    <li>• Use of approved blueprint systems for design interpretation and field coordination</li>
    <li>• Adherence to safety, environmental, and social safeguards</li>
    <li>• Alignment with donor procurement and oversight requirements</li>
    <li>• Participation in digital field reporting and monitoring systems</li>
    <li>• Transparent change management and variation request procedures</li>
    <li>• Mandatory daily reporting for high‑risk or critical path activities</li>
  </ul>

  <p class="mt-4 text-xs text-slate-400">
    All subcontractors must sign the OSA prior to mobilization. Non‑compliance may result in corrective actions,
    suspension of works, or removal from the project.
  </p>
</section>

<!-- OSA Standard: OSHA Compliance Requirement -->
<section class="border border-slate-800 bg-slate-900/60 rounded-xl p-6">
  <h3 class="text-sm font-semibold text-slate-100">OSA Standard: OSHA Compliance</h3>
  <p class="mt-2 text-xs text-slate-400">
    All subcontractors must comply with OSHA training requirements as part of the Operational Standards
    Agreement (OSA). In accordance with West Virginia state law (WV Code §21‑3‑22), any worker performing
    labor on a public improvement project valued at $500,000 or more must complete OSHA 10‑hour training
    prior to mobilization.
  </p>

  <ul class="mt-4 space-y-1 text-xs text-slate-400">
    <li>• Proof of OSHA 10‑hour training required for all field personnel</li>
    <li>• OSHA 30‑hour training required for supervisors, foremen, and site leads</li>
    <li>• Training certificates must be submitted before site access is granted</li>
    <li>• Non‑compliant personnel will be removed from the project immediately</li>
    <li>• Subcontractors are responsible for maintaining valid training records</li>
    <li>• Random compliance checks may be conducted at any time</li>
  </ul>

  <p class="mt-4 text-xs text-slate-400">
    These requirements ensure safe, compliant, and legally aligned operations across all infrastructure
    sectors, including transport, water, energy, mining, and public facilities.
  </p>
</section>

Operational Standards Agreement (OSA)
-------------------------------------

1. OSHA & WV Code Compliance
- OSHA 10-hour training required for all field workers.
- OSHA 30-hour training required for supervisors.
- WV Code §21‑3‑22 applies to public improvement projects exceeding $500,000.
- Training certificates must be submitted prior to mobilization.

2. Safety Requirements
- Daily toolbox talks.
- Job Hazard Analysis (JHA) for all tasks.
- Incident and near-miss reporting.
- Emergency response plan compliance.

3. Documentation Requirements
- Daily reports.
- QC/QA evidence.
- Equipment certifications.
- Attendance logs.

4. Digital Systems Requirements
- Mandatory use of digital reporting tools.
- Blueprint overlays for hazard mapping.
- Real-time monitoring where applicable.

5. Technical & Performance Standards
- Compliance with PMO workflows.
- Adherence to procurement and oversight frameworks.
- Transparent change management.
- Performance monitoring and corrective actions.

6. Enforcement
- Non-compliant personnel removed from site.
- Subcontractors responsible for maintaining valid records.
- Random compliance checks may be conducted.

GLOBAL INFRASTRUCTURE OPERATING SYSTEM (GIOS)
Overview
The Global Infrastructure Operating System (GIOS) is a unified delivery framework integrating PMO governance,
contractor oversight, safety management, blueprint systems, digital twin architecture, and compliance workflows
into a single defensible operating model.

Core Components
1. PMO Governance
   - Approvals, workflows, procurement pathways
   - Reporting cycles and audit trails
   - Contractor oversight integration

2. Contractor Oversight
   - Daily reporting and QC/QA evidence
   - Safety compliance (OSHA, WV Code §21‑3‑22)
   - U.S. sourcing alignment for eligible procurements
   - Performance scorecards and risk index

3. Safety Management System
   - JHAs, toolbox talks, incident logs
   - Hazard mapping and blueprint overlays
   - Training and competency tracking

4. Blueprint Systems
   - ANSYS-style engineering environments
   - Structural, civil, pipeline, mining, water, energy, and safety overlays
   - Blueprint-linked annotations and evidence

5. Digital Twin Architecture
   - GIS layers, sensor networks, real-time monitoring
   - Blueprint mesh integration
   - Alerts, thresholds, and performance indicators

6. Compliance Ecosystem
   - Buy American alignment
   - Sourcing verification workflow
   - Procurement evidence archive
   - Compliance dashboards and command center

7. Data & API Layer
   - Compliance API
   - Authentication and access control
   - Event streaming and normalization

Purpose
GIOS provides a unified, audit-ready, engineering-grade operating system for infrastructure delivery across
transport, water, energy, mining, and public facility sectors.

infrastructure sectors.

- Mining (shafts, tunnels, ventilation)
- Water (treatment, distribution, pressure maps)

- Blueprint-linked evidence trails

- Compliance API
